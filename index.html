The Leyland tractor after being pressure cleaned by Michael Hack
Great news from Tanzania! Both tractors that were carefully loaded into the container have successfully arrived in Tanzania on Christmas Day. This marks a significant milestone for our agricultural development efforts in the region and will dramatically improve our ability to support local farmers.

The container, which departed on December 2nd, included:

A refurbished Leyland tractor, thoroughly pressure-cleaned by Michael Hack
A small Ford tractor to support farming operations
Essential agricultural equipment for local farmers
Spare parts and maintenance tools
Thanks to the dedicated efforts of Michael Hack, Gray Blattmann, and Tim Wright who worked tirelessly to prepare and load the container, these valuable resources are now available to support our agricultural initiatives.

February 2025 Goals
In February 2025, we anticipate achieving the following milestones:

Training local farmers at the college
Finalizing the curriculum for farmer education
Training James and John in using our large planter
Developing the best strategy for farmer training and outreach
Assembling the tractors for immediate use
Building a 3-row planter and tractor trailer
Digging a well on the Agape farm
Setting up sprayers and calibrating equipment for planting
Current Challenges

While this is exciting progress, we still face some challenges. The Fordson tractor we purchased needs an injector pump replacement, which will take time. This meant we had to purchase another tractor, depleting our budget funds more than expected. Additionally, the fees to retrieve the container from the Tanzanian wharf were higher than anticipated.

THE NEEDS
One of our most pressing goals is to dig a well on the Agape land and install a submersible pump. This critical infrastructure would:

Help mitigate against drought conditions
Enable vegetable growing during the dry season
Support year-round agricultural training
Provide sustainable water access for the community
The estimated cost for the well and pump is $5,000, with good water available about 60 feet down.

Additionally, we need funds for accommodation and meals during our six-week stay, plus funds for immediate in-the-moment purchase of parts and other supplies to complete this next leg of the project.

The Budget and Our Vision

Thank you to the many who faithfully give what they can to the project. Budgeting for spending is always a challenge, and the best help is having regular monthly donors. No matter how insignificant the amounts look, they all add up over the year and give us a base from which to project our annual budget.

We are now ready to take on new villages and engage our team in farmer training. As part of their training at Agape College, pastors/leaders/students who wish to will receive training in Conservation Agriculture. The course we use is called Foundations for Farming.

In the last newsletter (October 2024), we mentioned that we need to arrange a budget for the college course and acquire a decent printer for the college to provide our documents to the students. It often requires feeding them meals and providing accommodation out in the villages.

Thank you for your continued support of the Tiamoyo Project.

- The Tiamoyo Team
